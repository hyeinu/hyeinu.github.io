X Factor- Do I have what it takes?

At my previous job I was fortunate enough to be put into situations that made me face my fears and grow as an individual. There are just so many lessons I learned during that period of my life which have benefited me.

But in my opinion the three most important skills I honed were the following:

1. Task Management

With any task I find myself attempting I always try to apply these five steps -

  • Plan - always develop a clear Point A, Point B, and how to get between those two points.
  • Prepare - proper preparation reduces the chances of complications.
  • Prioritize - every task has multiple parts to it, but it is important to recognize which parts contribute most to the optimal progression of the task.
  • Problem Solve - even with proper preparation there will always be problems. When a problem occurs it is important to stay calm and to try and figure a solution.
  • Progress - at the end of any day there needs to be progress towards a conclusion.

2. Organization

I would not be able to accomplish any task with just those five steps, in order for success I need to be organized. I have been accused of having an obsessive compulsive disorder; however, it is simply that I find organization plays a key role in my ability to assign myself tasks and preforming those tasks.

3. Communication

I have never had a problem with talking; but holding a casual conversation and imparting my thoughts & ideas are two very different skills. While working internationally with people whose primary language was not English it was important that I learned how to clearly express myself in order to accomplish my assignment. Likewise being able to accurately convey my vision is beneficial in this field.
