X Factor

At my previous job I was fortunate enough to be put into situations to made me face my fears and grow as an individual. There are just so many lessons I learned during that period of my life which have benefited me in the long-term. But in my opinion the three most important skills I honed were the following:

1. Dealing with a task

With any task I find myself attempting I always try to remember these five steps -

  • Plan - always develop a clear Point A, Point B, and how to get between those two points.
  • Prepare - proper preparation reduces the chances of complications
  • Prioritize - every task has multiple parts to it, but it is important to recognize which parts contribute most to the optimal progression of the task.
  • Problem Solve - even with proper preparation there will always be problems. When a problem occurs it is important to remember the world is not ending and to try and figure a way out.
  • Progress - at the end of any day there needs to be progress to eventually complete a task.

    • 2. Organization

    The five steps I listed above means nothing without organzation. Organization plays a key role in my ability to assign myself tasks and getting those tasks done.

    3. Communication

    I have never had a problem with talking but holding a casual conversation and imparting my thoughts and ideas are two very different skills. And I believe that being able to express oneself in such a manner is helpful to an individual in this field.
